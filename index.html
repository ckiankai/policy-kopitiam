Singapore healthcare system
MediShield Life → ISP → Riders

In Singapore, it helps to think in layers rather than “one plan”. The layers stack, they are parts of one system, not simply competing products.

Base layer: MediShield Life
National basic health insurance for large hospital bills and certain treatments, designed with limits and cost-sharing.
Optional layer: Integrated Shield Plans (ISP)
Private insurer coverage that sits on top of MediShield Life, often offering higher coverage for certain ward classes, subject to policy terms.
Optional add-on: Riders
Add-ons that often change how much you pay out of pocket, mainly through deductible and co-pay mechanics, sometimes with caps and conditions.

Partial subsidies and cost-sharing are normal here. Insurance rarely means “pays everything”, it usually means “reduces disruption”.

Riders, deductible vs co-pay (trade-offs)

Riders often change how costs are shared between you and the insurer. They do not remove all friction, they reshape it.

Two mechanics to know

  • Deductible, you pay the first fixed amount before insurance starts paying.
  • Co-pay, after deductible, you pay a percentage of the remaining eligible bill.

Simplified for understanding mechanics. Real policies can include caps, limits, and conditions.

Before you buy anything

These are not “trick questions”. They’re questions that help you understand what you are committing to.

Ask yourself first

  • what am i trying to protect, health bills, income, dependants, liabilities
  • if this risk happened this year, what would break financially
  • what coverage do i already have (MediShield, CPF, employer, existing policies)
  • can i afford this premium not just now, but later
  • if my income dropped, would this policy become a burden

Ask before signing

  • what exactly triggers a claim
  • what situations are excluded
  • what do i still pay even after insurance
  • what changes over time (premium, coverage, conditions)
  • can i explain this to someone else in 60 seconds

If you can’t describe the moving parts clearly, you don’t yet know what you’re buying.

Glossary

You will see some terms underlined throughout the site. Hover on desktop or tap on mobile for a quick definition.

Premium

The amount you pay to keep coverage active (monthly or yearly).

Deductible

The first fixed amount you pay before insurance starts paying (simplified).

Co-pay

The percentage you pay after deductible, the insurer pays the rest (simplified).

Rider

An add-on that changes or extends a base policy, often by adjusting benefits or cost-sharing.

Exclusion

Situations or conditions the policy does not cover.

Coverage

What the policy pays for, under what conditions, and up to what limits.

How to know when you’ve done enough

Adequacy is not “everything covered”. Adequacy is asking whether a bad year would permanently derail your life plans. Insurance is not meant to remove all inconvenience, discomfort, or uncertainty. It exists to prevent one unlikely but severe event from locking you into years of recovery you never planned for. If a single event would rewrite your trajectory entirely, that is usually where insurance has a role.
